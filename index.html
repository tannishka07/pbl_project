मन saathi – Mental Wellness App for Students

Project Statement:
Can a student-centric digital platform integrate mood tracking, music therapy, journaling, relaxation tools, and motivational support to provide private, accessible, and effective mental wellness support for students?

Problem Statement

College students face increasing levels of stress, anxiety, and emotional burnout due to academic pressure, social expectations, and lifestyle imbalance. Existing mental health applications are often paid, lack personalization, or focus only on limited features such as meditation or chat support. Students need a private, accessible, and student-focused platform integrating mood tracking, music therapy, journaling, relaxation tools, and motivational support.

  • Increasing stress and anxiety among students
  • Lack of affordable mental wellness solutions
  • Limited personalization in existing apps
  • Stigma and hesitation in seeking therapy

Literature Review / Market Research

Apps like Wysa provide AI chatbot mental health support. Headspace and Calm offer meditation tools but are paid. Endel provides personalized music but is not student-focused. WHO and NIMHANS research shows music therapy helps reduce anxiety and improve concentration. However, there is no unified student-focused mental wellness platform.

Research Gap / Innovation

Existing apps focus on individual features such as meditation, chatbots, or music. This project creates a unified student-centric mental wellness platform integrating mood tracking, music therapy, journaling, relaxation tools, motivational content, and progress tracking.

System Methodology

Input Data

User mood entries, journaling input, relaxation activity usage, and music preferences collected through the app.

System Architecture

Mood tracking analyzes emotions and recommends personalized music, journaling tools, relaxation exercises, and motivational content.

Application Execution

Software interface provides mood tracking, music therapy, journaling, relaxation tools, and progress dashboard.

Results & Analysis

Student Wellness Improvement
85%

Students using the platform showed improved emotional awareness, reduced stress, and increased engagement in mental wellness activities.
